üìä What Are PFAS?

PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) are a large group of approximately 10,000 man-made chemicals known for their extreme environmental persistence, earning them the nickname "forever chemicals." While their persistence has long been recognized, recent decades have shed light on other serious concerns, including:

  • Bioaccumulation in living organisms
  • High mobility in water, soil, and air
  • Long-range environmental transport
  • (Eco)toxicological effects on humans and ecosystems
